We conducted experiments with data collected from eight amputees to test the accuracy of a control system that could be used in functional and non-functional tasks. The findings support the development of wearable devices that can provide functional context for these individuals.

Atrial fibrillation is one of the most frequently diagnosed and most common conditions, often involving older age groups. It is an arrhythmia whose treatment today is very careful and it allows in almost all cases, if it is correctly regulated to prevent stroke and heart failure.

Atrial fibrillation can cause more than 150 heartbeats per minute. This is known as tachyarrhythmia. Atrial fibrillation is manifested by accelerated heartbeats, palpitations that last for several hours or more. Other symptoms may include Other symptoms may be a feeling of tightness or pressure in the chest, fatigue or difficulty breathing difficulties. The problems may disappear when the acute attacks develop into permanent atrial fibrillation.

Atrial fibrillation can occur without apparent cause. However, However, aging and a family predisposition favour its occurrence. Other factors that can be acted upon are frequently present: obesity, heart problems, sleep apnea.
